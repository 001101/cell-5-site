web

Our roots are in web development and our team are passionate about it. We understand that each customers needs are different and this is why we offer a free consultation to adapt your package to meet your needs.

Our base package is Design & Build, and starts from £350. This includes:

  • the design, development and deployment of your new website.
  • mobile-first testing so your site is guaranteed to work on mobile.
  • on-site s.e.o to ensure the defaults in your site are correctly tailored to maximize traffic
  • an inclusive process of design and development, where you get to review mulitple previews and offer feedback

This package can then be extended with these optional extras:

  • + One-off £50 - basic webmaster training for you, suitable for the tech savvy entrepeneur who has a few hours to maintain their site on an ongoing basis.
  • + Monthly £5 - monthly content update and site upgrade, and annual up-lift where you get a chance to make small enhancements and we make sure your website stays current.
  • + Monthly £5 - secure and fast hosting on our managed cloud servers

mobile

We design, build, test, and publish mobile applications that effectively reach and engage your target customers.

As experts in mobile development, we understand the trade-offs between fully native mobile applications, hybrid solutions, and mobile-first web designs in terms of cost, effectiveness, and capability.

Our team has a wealth of expertise in developing for Android and iOS, utilizing technologies such as ObjectiveC, Swift, Java, and ReactNative, and we can advise on which stack suits your goals.

Prices for mobile application development start at £750. Get in touch to arrange a free initial consultation and estimation session. If you think your needs might be better met with a web-site, all our web applications are built to be mobile-first; see our web offerings to find out more.

legacy code uplift

Great design today is tomorrow’s legacy.

The team has a wealth of experience at large financial institutions - we’ve seen our fair share of legacy code, or code that needed uplifting for the new scaling demands.
